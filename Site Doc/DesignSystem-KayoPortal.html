08 — Design Principles

What Guides Every Decision

Five rules. Any design choice that breaks one of these is wrong, regardless of how good it looks.

Speed is the feature.
The portal exists to get you to your project in under 5 seconds. Every animation, every hover state, every piece of information on a card is measured against that outcome. Nothing decorative.
Cards are the interface.
There is no navigation, no search, no sidebar. The Bento Grid is the entire UX. Cards must communicate name, type, and status at a glance — before any hover or interaction.
Hover reveals — it doesn't replace.
The base card state must be complete and useful. Hover reveals the description and launch arrow, but a card stripped of its hover state should still be perfectly readable and identifiable.
Dark means dark.
True black (#000000), not dark grey. No light mode. No toggle. The Obsidian Minimalist aesthetic only works at maximum contrast — any compromise on the background colour breaks the glass card effect.
Adding a project must take under 10 minutes.
The portal is only as useful as it is current. If adding a new card requires touching more than one file, the system is too complex. config/projects.ts is the single source of truth — push, and it's live.
